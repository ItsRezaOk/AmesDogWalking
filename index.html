Physical Health & Weight Control

  • Daily activity target: Dogs need approximately 30 minutes of physical activity daily, adjusted for breed, age, and health status[1]
  • Weight management: About 59% of U.S. dogs are overweight or obese. Consistent walking supports healthy weight and joint function[3]
  • Puppy-safe exercise: Young dogs need roughly 5 minutes per month of age per session, gradually increasing as they mature[2]

Mental Enrichment & Behavior

  • Stress reduction: Sniffing and exploring outdoors provides essential mental enrichment that reduces anxiety and improves behavior[4]
  • Routine benefits: Consistent exercise helps establish healthy sleep patterns and reduces destructive behaviors
  • Weather-smart approach: Iowa's climate requires careful attention to heat protection, paw safety, and proper hydration[5]

Frequently Asked Questions

How do you handle Iowa weather extremes?

I monitor temperature closely and adjust walks accordingly. In summer, I avoid peak heat hours and check pavement temperature. In winter, I watch for ice and salt irritation, and keep walks shorter in extreme cold. Dogs always have access to fresh water, and I'll recommend indoor alternatives during severe weather[5].

What about puppies? How long should their walks be?

Puppies need gentle, age-appropriate exercise. I follow the guideline of roughly 5 minutes per month of age per session[2]. A 4-month-old puppy would get about 20 minutes of activity, focusing more on exploration and socialization than intense exercise. We gradually increase duration as they grow.

My dog is anxious or pulls on walks. Can you help?

Absolutely. I use positive, patient techniques for anxious or reactive dogs. This includes letting them set the pace for sniffing (which reduces stress), using consistent routes until they're comfortable, and working on gentle leash manners. Many behavioral issues improve with regular, low-pressure exercise[4].

Do you customize walks for different breeds and ages?

Yes, every dog gets individualized attention. High-energy breeds might get more vigorous exercise, while older dogs or brachycephalic (flat-faced) breeds get gentler, shorter walks with more rest breaks. I work with you to understand your dog's specific needs and adjust accordingly[1].

What if I need to cancel or reschedule?

I understand that schedules change. For a la carte bookings, just give me 4 hours notice when possible. Subscription members get 2 monthly rollovers, so missed walks can be made up later in the month. Emergency cancellations are always accommodated without penalty.
